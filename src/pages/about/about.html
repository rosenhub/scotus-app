ABOUT

The Supreme Court of the United States
is also known as SCOTUS. It is one of three main branches of the US Government.


SCOTUS consists of the Chief Justice and eight Associate Justices. Power to nominate Justices is vested in the President with the advice and consent of the US Senate.


It was founded in 1790 under Article III of the US Constitution and states that the Justices shall hold their offices during good behaviour and receive compensation for their services.


The Court Building is open to the public Monday to Friday from 9am to 4:30pm.
* Closed on Federal holidays.
