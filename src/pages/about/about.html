The Supreme Court of the United States is also known as SCOTUS. It is one of three main branches of the US Government.


SCOTUS consists of the Chief Justice and eight Associate Justices. Power to nominate Justices is vested in the President with the advice and consent of the US Senate.


The Court Building is open to the public Monday to Friday from 9am to 4:30pm.
* Closed on Federal holidays.
