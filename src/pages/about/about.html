ABOUT

The Supreme Court of the United States is also known as SCOTUS and it's one of three main branches of Government along with the Legislative and Executive branches.

The Court was founded in 1790 under Article III of the United States Constitution. It consists of the Chief Justice and eight Associate Justices.

The power to nominate Justices is vested with the President of the United States along with the advice and consent of the Senate.

The Court Building is free to visit and open Monday to Friday from 9am to 4:30pm.

  * Closed on Federal Holidays.
